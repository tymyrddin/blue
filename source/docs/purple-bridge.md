# Crossing to purple

Blue is the controls: what to turn on, what to harden, what to watch. Purple is the threat modelling
and the practices around it, working out what is worth defending, against whom, and how anyone would
know it had failed. The two are kept apart on purpose. A control page that also argued its own threat
model would be marking its own homework.

The framing for all of it lives on the purple side: [Asimov's three Foundations](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/asimov/),
the [second foundation](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/foundations/) nobody asked for, and
[purple teaming](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/purple/) as the third option that makes both
uncomfortable. This page is the crossing: for each blue section, where the matching thinking sits.

## The field

- Home. The stack here stops the busy-and-distracted failure; the human angle and a first threat
  model are worked out in purple [social engineering](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/social-engineering/)
  and [threat modelling](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/threat-modelling/).
- DIY servers. These pages name the controls; purple [threat modelling](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/threat-modelling/)
  and [systems architecture](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/systems-architecture/) name the threats
  those controls address, and the ones they do not.
- Active defence. Deception and auto-blocking earn their place only against a mapped attacker, so the
  groundwork is purple [attack-path work](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/threat-modelling/); what
  trips, and what happens next, is [incident response](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/incident-response/).
- Dev. Build-side controls; the engineering they sit in, and the newer exposure, are purple
  [systems engineering](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/systems-engineering/),
  [AI security](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/ai-security/), and
  [cloud](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/cloud/).
- OT and ICS. Plant-floor controls; the architecture they live in, and the continuity question when
  one fails, are purple [systems architecture](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/systems-architecture/)
  and [continuity](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/continuity/).
- Counter moves. Attacker techniques from the defender's view; the attacker gets modelled first in
  purple [threat modelling](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/threat-modelling/) and
  [social engineering](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/social-engineering/).

## Fictional premises

- Golem Trust Computing. A consultancy's controls; the assessment, risk, and engagement practice the
  same clients need are purple [audits](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/audits/),
  [risk management](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/risk-management/),
  [engagement](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/engagement/), and
  [purple teaming](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/purple/).
- Civic Defence Establishment. The institution that stops conditions rather than crimes; purple holds
  the advisory [thirteenth chair](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/thirteen/) in the room, the
  [administrative attack surface](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/admin-surface/) it guards, and the
  [Purple Lantern](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/lantern/) exercises the red-lantern work plays
  against.
- Office of Civil Surveys. Routes, flows, and signal paths read as intelligence; purple's analytical
  [workshops](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/workshops/) are the same habit of mind, formalised.
- Civil Observers' Society. Volunteer research and tool-building under cover of responsible
  disclosure; the practice ground, and the civil-liberties question disclosure tends to raise, are
  purple [CTF Foundry](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/ctfs/) and the
  [MCLU](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/mclu/).
- Bewildered Beasts. NGO security on a patchwork budget; responding, prioritising, and carrying on
  when something gives are purple [incident response](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/incident-response/),
  [risk management](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/risk-management/), and
  [continuity](https://purple.tymyrddin.dev/docs/continuity/).
