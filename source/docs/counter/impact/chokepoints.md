# When geography is the wall

The crisis that dramatises this: [Manoeuvre Narrow Margin](../../scenarios/supply-chokepoints.md).

Every other impact family assumes the loss happens to something digital. This one ignores the digital
layer entirely. The clacks flash, the golems wait, and the city still starves, because the physical
inputs they were built to process are stuck five miles out in the rain, sorted into long stationary
lines by the width of a road. The adversary needs no password and no poisoned ledger, only three
things that cannot pass each other in a narrow space.

## The shape of it

The weaponisation of physical constraint. A supply line optimised for efficiency runs through a small
number of irreplaceable points: a pass, a river mouth, a single bridge, a customs gate. Each is a
concentration of throughput rather than of compute, and each fails the same way, by being blocked
rather than broken. The blockage can be a shattered axle, a grounded barge, or a compliance
requirement amended three days ago. None of it looks like an attack, and all of it moves the supplies
from in transit to stationary, which is the only state that does the work.

## Where it shows

The early signals read as administrative friction and bad luck. A wagon breaks down where the cutting
narrows. A barge grounds on a mudbank. A customs queue slows because the paperwork changed. Each has
an innocent reading, and the innocent readings are free, until the locations line up: the blockages
sit at exactly the points where the geography offers no alternative, and the timing is too tidy to be
weather. The digital monitoring stays green throughout, because nothing digital is wrong.

## Countermoves

Clearance against preservation: a grounded barge can be cleared by force in minutes, at the cost of
the asset, the goodwill of whoever owned it, and a public signal that the holder is panicking. The
secondary-route exposure: rerouting onto unmonitored alternative lanes relieves the choke and, in the
same motion, maps the emergency infrastructure for whoever is watching, and those routes can be
pre-seeded with delays of their own. The administrative choke: a compliance loop genuine in
everything but intent stalls movement legally, so breaking it takes someone senior enough to sign an
override the registries will later treat as the violation. Resilience here is width, redundancy in the
physical layer that does nothing on an ordinary day and is the whole of the defence on a bad one.
